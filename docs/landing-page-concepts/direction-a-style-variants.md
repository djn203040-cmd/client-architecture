# Direction A — Station 1 Hero: Style Variants

Three style explorations of the same building. Same six-floor structure, same per-floor contents, same brand palette anchor. Goal: generate one of each in Nano Banana Pro, then lock the winning style and re-render Stations 2–6 against it.

**Shared building spec (identical across all three variants):**
- Six-story narrow tall townhouse, facade peeled away, all six floors visible at once
- Building occupies center 35% of frame width, fills ~90% of frame height
- Each floor approximately 1/6 of the total building height
- Continuous spiral staircase up the left side, threading all six floors
- 16:9, 2880×1620
- Per-floor contents (top to bottom):
  - Floor 6: rooftop **Calendar Reception** — brass-framed skylight set into the roof, a BURNT-ORANGE wax-sealed envelope hovering just above the skylight (the *only* saturated color in the scene), forest-green velvet chair, brass side table, row of seven small brass crests on the back wall
  - Floor 5: **Voice Library** — floor-to-ceiling shelves of leather-bound books, brass reading rail, marble plinth with a softly glowing tablet
  - Floor 4: **Drafting Room** — slender forest-green writing desk, **LIT** brass desk lamp casting a warm pool of light on a sheet of cream paper, single forest velvet chair
  - Floor 3: **Approval Hall** — three identical forest-green panelled doors with brass nameplates above. **Middle door slightly open** with a soft burnt-orange glow spilling across the floor
  - Floor 2: **Mail Hall** — wide hallway with a brass mail chute in the cream wall, small wooden bench, framed picture
  - Ground Floor: **Entrance** — heavy forest-green front door slightly ajar, warm light spilling out, small engraved brass plaque above reading "Sonorous Digital"

## Variant 1 — Photoreal Architectural Film

> A real building, photographed mid-construction with one wall removed. Architectural-visualization studio quality.

**Prompt:**

> Photorealistic architectural cross-section photograph of a six-story narrow tall townhouse with the front facade removed, all six interior floors visible simultaneously. Style: high-end architectural visualization in the manner of HOK, Foster + Partners, or Bjarke Ingels Group portfolio renders. Photographed as if with a Hasselblad medium-format camera on Kodak Portra 400 film, subtle natural film grain throughout.
>
> The building is tall and narrow, occupying the center 35% of the frame width, filling 90% of the frame height. Six clearly distinct floors of equal height stacked vertically, each floor approximately 1/6 of the total building height. A continuous dark-wood spiral staircase with brass railings threads up the left side, connecting all floors.
>
> Materials rendered with photographic realism: cream lime-washed plaster walls with subtle texture and age, dark forest-green stained oak floorboards with visible grain, thin brushed-brass inlay strips between floors with realistic micro-scratches and patina, forest-green painted timber doors with aged brass hardware, tall arched windows with real refractive glass.
>
> Lighting: hard raking golden-hour sun streaming in from the upper-right at approximately 5:30pm in late September, casting long warm rectangular light shafts across each floor and creating crisp angular shadows. Realistic atmospheric haze visible in the warmest beams. Background is a soft cream-paper backdrop with a subtle realistic contact shadow beneath the building.
>
> Floor 6 (top): a rooftop reception room with a brass-framed glass skylight set into the flat roof. A burnt-orange wax-sealed envelope hovers just above the skylight, slightly tilted, mid-drop — this envelope is the only saturated color in the entire scene. Inside: a forest-green velvet armchair, a brass side table, a row of seven small brass crests on the back wall.
>
> Floor 5: a voice library with floor-to-ceiling oak shelves of leather-bound books, a brass reading rail, and a small marble plinth at center holding a softly glowing translucent tablet.
>
> Floor 4: a drafting room with a slender forest-green writing desk centered, a LIT brass desk lamp casting a warm tungsten pool of light onto a single sheet of cream paper on the desk.
>
> Floor 3: an approval hall with three forest-green panelled doors in a row, each with a brass nameplate above. The middle door is slightly ajar with a soft burnt-orange glow spilling out across the dark oak floor.
>
> Floor 2: a mail hall with a brass mail chute mounted in the cream wall, a small wooden bench beneath it, a framed picture on the opposite wall.
>
> Ground Floor: a heavy forest-green front door slightly ajar with warm light spilling outward. A small engraved brass plaque above the door reads "Sonorous Digital" in delicate serif.
>
> No people. No UI overlays. No text other than the Sonorous Digital plaque. Ultra-detailed photorealistic materials. 16:9, 2880×1620.

## Variant 2 — Tactile Handmade Paper Model

> A real handcrafted architectural model, photographed in a studio. The hand of the maker is visible in the materials.

**Prompt:**

> Photograph of a handcrafted architectural scale model of a six-story narrow tall townhouse with the front facade removed, all six interior floors visible simultaneously. Style: in the manner of MIT architecture thesis models, Helen Musselwhite paper-cut artworks, and Olafur Eliasson studio dioramas. The model is clearly hand-made from cut paper, cardstock, and balsa wood — visible cut edges, slight paper grain, subtle imperfections that read as human craftsmanship.
>
> The model occupies the center 35% of frame width and fills 90% of frame height. Six clearly distinct floors of equal height, each floor approximately 1/6 of the total building height. Tiny burnt-brass pins join the floor plates at the corners — visible as small craft details. A continuous spiral staircase made of layered balsa wood with fine brass-wire railings threads up the left side.
>
> Materials: walls are cream cardstock with visible paper grain and slightly uneven cut edges. Floor plates are forest-green painted balsa wood, the paint slightly textured by brush strokes. Trim and inlays are real thin brass strips, the brass slightly oxidized and warm. Doors are tiny pieces of painted balsa with miniature real brass hardware. Books on the library shelves are individual hand-cut paper pieces. The model rests on a sheet of warm cream paper as backdrop.
>
> Lighting: soft diffused studio light from a north-facing window above and to the left at 4000K, creating gentle shadows beneath each cantilevered floor plate. Subtle warm fill from a tungsten bulb on the right. Shallow contact shadows beneath the entire model. No harshness, no gloss.
>
> Floor 6 (top): a rooftop reception with a tiny brass-framed glass skylight made of real glass set into the cardboard roof. A burnt-orange wax-sealed envelope (tiny, made of real cardstock and real wax) hovers just above the skylight on a nearly-invisible thread — this is the only saturated color in the entire scene. Inside: a miniature forest-green velvet armchair (felted velvet), a brass side table (real brass), a row of seven tiny brass crests glued to the back wall.
>
> Floor 5: a voice library with miniature floor-to-ceiling oak shelves filled with individual hand-cut paper book spines, a thin brass wire reading rail, and a small white marble cube plinth holding a softly glowing rice-paper tablet lit from within by a tiny LED.
>
> Floor 4: a drafting room with a tiny forest-green writing desk made of balsa, a working miniature brass desk lamp (LIT, casting a real warm pool of tungsten light onto a sheet of cream paper on the desk).
>
> Floor 3: an approval hall with three identical forest-green panelled doors made of balsa, each with a tiny brass nameplate above. The middle door is propped slightly open with a tiny LED behind it casting a soft burnt-orange glow across the floor plate.
>
> Floor 2: a mail hall with a tiny brass mail chute set into the cardstock wall, a balsa wood bench beneath it, a miniature framed picture on the opposite wall.
>
> Ground Floor: a heavy forest-green front door slightly ajar with warm light spilling outward from a tiny LED. A small engraved brass plaque above the door reads "Sonorous Digital" — the engraving is real and visible at this scale.

## Variant 3 — Painterly 3D (Klaus / Aardman warmth)

> A frame from a high-end stop-motion animated feature. Stylized geometry, painterly textures, characterful lighting.

**Prompt:**

> A single frame from a high-end stop-motion animated film, depicting an architectural cross-section of a six-story narrow tall townhouse with the front facade removed, all six interior floors visible at once. Visual style in the manner of the 2019 Netflix film "Klaus" (Sergio Pablos / SPA Studios), Aardman Animation's "Early Man", and the meticulous set design of Wes Anderson's "Isle of Dogs". Painterly hand-finished textures, slight characterful exaggeration in proportions, warm and crafted.
>
> The building occupies the center 35% of frame width, fills 90% of frame height. Six clearly distinct floors of equal height, each floor approximately 1/6 of the total height. A continuous spiral staircase with brass railings threads up the left side connecting all floors. The geometry is slightly stylized — wider plank floorboards than realistic, slightly exaggerated brass details, charming small imperfections — but never cartoonish.
>
> Materials with painterly hand-finished textures: cream lime-washed walls with visible brush strokes in the paint, dark forest-green floorboards with stylized wood grain that reads as painted not photographed, warm tactile brass details with hand-rendered highlights, forest-green painted doors with subtle storybook charm.
>
> Lighting: warm stylized golden-hour glow, soft painterly bloom, no harsh shadows, warm tungsten key from the upper right with a subtle cool fill from the left. Each lit element has a slight halo. Background is a softly painted cream wash with a stylized warm contact shadow beneath the building.

> Floor 6 (top): a rooftop calendar reception with a charming brass-framed skylight. > Floor 3: an approval hall with three forest-green panelled doors in a row with brass nameplates. The middle door stands slightly open with a soft burnt-orange glow spilling out, rendered as a warm painterly halo across the floor.

> Floor 2: a mail hall with a brass mail chute, a small wooden bench, a framed picture on the wall — all rendered with characterful stop-motion charm.
>
> Ground Floor: a heavy forest-green front door slightly ajar with warm light spilling out. A small engraved brass plaque above the door reads "Sonorous Digital" in storybook serif.

4. Pick the winner — that locks the style for Stations 2–6
5. I'll update `direction-a-cross-section.md` to bake the winning style language into Stations 2–6 prompts so the building stays visually identical across all six stations
