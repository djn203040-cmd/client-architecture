# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Coaches — independent coaching professionals who review AI-generated follow-up emails, approve or adjust drafts, manage leads from post-call intake, and monitor their outreach sequences. They are not technical. They use this tool in short focused sessions, often between calls or at end of day. Daniel (the operator) accesses the admin view at /admin to manage all coaches and system health.

## Product Purpose

A managed software service that runs the follow-up system for coaching businesses. The AI drafts emails in each coach's voice, surfaces them for approval 24 hours before send, and executes the sequence automatically. The coach's job is to stay in the loop, not to operate the system. Success means a coach can review and approve their week's outreach in under 10 minutes, confident the tone sounds like them.

## Brand Personality

Warm, assured, premium. The interface should feel like a well-made tool that a trusted colleague built specifically for you — not a generic SaaS product. Tone: calm authority, not hustle-culture energy. The three words: warm, precise, elevated.

## Anti-references

- Neon green / dark purple / tech-bro color palettes
- Chart-heavy "analytics" dashboards (coaches are not finance teams)
- Generic SaaS cream-and-blue (HubSpot, Salesforce aesthetic)
- Startup-motivational copy ("Supercharge your pipeline!")
- Heavy gradients and glow effects
- Cold, clinical interfaces that feel like B2B enterprise software

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears into the task.** Coaches come to approve drafts and check leads, not to admire the UI. Every interaction should be obvious on first sight.
2. **Warmth is functional.** The warm color palette and soft surfaces aren't decoration — they reduce the visual stress of review work done in short windows.
3. **Premium means restraint.** No hero metrics. No decoration for its own sake. A glass card earns its blur when it creates hierarchy, not by default.
4. **Copy is part of the design.** Every label, empty state, and locked-module CTA is written to the same standard as the product itself — specific, confident, never generic.
5. **Simple scales.** The dashboard at launch has two metrics. That is correct. Add complexity only when the workflow demands it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All interactive elements keyboard-accessible with visible focus indicators. Minimum 44px touch targets on mobile. Both dark and light modes fully supported — neither is a fallback.
